Process

Design is about more than creating a good-looking final product. The process of taking an idea from inception to completion is where a design becomes something more: a solution.

Throughout my process I make very conscious and intentional design decisions that resolve usability challenges and improve the overall experience. Iterating within each step of my process allows me to produce a strong, well-developed design solution.

Research + Sketches:

I begin each project by reviewing the client brief to gain an in-depth understanding of their needs.

I then start to consider the site’s information architecture and explore the most efficient way to organize the flow from page to page.

Lastly, I begin to explore page layouts by creating quick thumbnails and sketches.

Wireframes:

Wireframing is where I work out the site’s hierarchical structure in a very clean and organized fashion.

These diagrams become a useful tool when communicating ideas to client prior to designing the look and feel of the site.

Design + Build:

Once all previous stages are solidified, I begin to visually bring the site to life. I enjoy revisiting my notes outlining the client’s brand and goals to fully implement both.

In this phase I test the design in various browsers and on different devices for usability flaws.

In the example, the initial design did not display any navigational options upon opening. Changes were made to correct this and to improve the site’s functionality.

Launch:

After the project goals are met, issues are resolved, and an intuitive user experience is produced, the final product is made live.
